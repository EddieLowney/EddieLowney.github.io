Welcome to my Website about Evolutionary Computing

Evolutionary computing is a field of computer science which seeks to imitate biological evolution to devise novel solutions which are not easily derived through more conventional, less random ways. The field of elements of evolutionary computing have been prevalent in the world of artifical intelligence since the 1950s and 1960s, but evolutionary computing only gained significant recognition in the 1990s. Much of the most prevalent work in evolutionary computing seeks to investigate biological evolutionary processes, but the field is by no means limited to the study of biological processes like populations of chromosomes. Most of the biological work seeks to directly model the biological processes of recombination, mutation, and selective pressure. One notable early example of non-biological applications is the work of Ingo Rechenberg and Hans-Paul Schewefel. In this work the two solved a fluid dynamics problem which could not be solved using traditional mathematical approaches. Today the field of evolutionary computing consists of many prominent techniques and approaches. Many modern approaches use agent-based modeling methods in which agents are generated and altered by the algorithm and then scored following each iteration. Depending on the approach used, low scoring agents are then removed and another iteration begins. On another page, I elaborate on basic implementaion of evolutionary computing.
